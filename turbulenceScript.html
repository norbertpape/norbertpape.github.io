written by Ben Woodard

Desktop

Turbulence. It feels quiet now, but I suspect we are still at its whim.

Things are falling apart. Disagreements over the nature of nature and thus also with the direction the project should take. Legal inquiries are made into the possibility of retracting one’s contributions to the work. There’s the whole problem of nature thinking about itself through us, that the nature of nature is always from one angle at a time.

Part of the team is still onboard, patching together fragments of materials to shape a container that could provide some sort of makeshift shelter, of hope, for us, but also for others to come.

The other part of the team has spun out of reach, telecommunication has come to a standstill. We are each alone in our remote outposts.

But the clouds are still passing, and the sound of an endless stream of vehicles is still there too. The gentle movement of the leaves in the wind is soothing. As I let my defenses down, I start to feel the warmth of sadness, and my mind drifts. I like looking at the clouds, through glass or on glass. Who doesn’t?

If things do keep falling apart, and I mean, properly apart, then how, I wonder, will our children’s children’s children know what it felt like when things were not apart. What would pictures mean to them, if they had never stood on a beach and felt the waves lap at their feet? What good would moving images be if these images only seem to reference themselves?

So we set out to capture the world not only as congealed moments in time, or of time, but also as sets of relations that could maybe some day be reactivated.

Turbulent archive - Earth

Computer Voice: Orbital drone 857, Sector 009
Begin playback.

Voice 2: Pings and pulses from a planet that some time ago we thought might be habitable. The first science team, all dead by now, started calling it ripple. The globe was almost entirely ocean but it was a dead one, no life. There seemed to be so much potential for life there but it’s only a tomb. So many fossils, expired bits of life, the whole planet is a container for the dead. But we were told to leave the drone there circling forever in orbit. Or, not forever, till its power cells fail to drink up enough solar radiation to keep it from succumbing to the gravity well. [sounds]

What is to be learned from the congealed bits of life now hardened into rock? The history of the planet, the history of life on the planet, the history of non-life, the history of the planet in its solar system...its just levels of compact exhaustion at this point. The world is a quiet world. Perhaps this message will itself deteriorate before it is heard, or read, or seen, or detected. This archive contains troves of data about lost forms of turbulence and other material contingencies which have become increasingly rare as the universe clocks down towards total entropy. Or, that’s my guess anyway. Tracking smaller and smaller movements here, sometimes out of boredom. My monkey brain, or my rational brain, wants to see every motion as a cause, with a direction, with an agent behind it. Many hands moving all the puzzle pieces of the planet around to make things make sense. But it’s just sand shifting in a pile but spread across multiple mediums: rock, water, sand, flesh, storm gusts, all forms of turbulence. Some are gentle, almost distracting, others are cataclysmic but leave little trace in memory, they erase the traces of their happening, and of other existences with it.

Nebula - Air

Computer Voice: Deep research drone 728, nebula x-2755, sector 0941
Begin Playback.

Voice 4: Patterns, intelligent patterns? Someone lost in the cloud, or is it the cloud itself?

There's been a few reports of non-corporeal life forms....complex energy patterns that exhibit agency, higher order intelligence. As if we had good definitions of those things yet. Yet, if not by now then when? Or the whole nebulous thing could be alive...novel chemical reactions from the leftovers of collapsed stars leading to some self augmenting system. Stable pockets of energy conservation...who cares if it’s in a meat-sack as long as it keeps itself going. But maybe it's just random, bits of plasma filaments igniting and we idiots think it's a neuron the size of a whale. I miss whales.

The cloud is slow like them, like much sea life, or maybe more like a jelly fish....a ballooning life. But it's a preview of the end’s end, - collapse, explosion, diffusion. The AGI living with me (if that’s living) is convinced it’s an artificial life form taking refuge in the remnant, hiding from the dark forest of organic life...waiting for this wet breathing carnival to pass into the past. Maybe non-biological life will find a loophole out of this return to the elements.

A pocket universe, a time machine, a dimensional jump?

But none of that....we thinned out our supplies too quickly....maybe our biological systems are… too fragile anyway.

Computer Voice: Proximity alert. Unidentified vessel approaching.
